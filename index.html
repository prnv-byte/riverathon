Riverathon

Revive. Restore. Rejuvenate.

Revive, Restore & Rejuvenate river using LiDaR data. This hackathon brought to you by Amity School of Engineering & Technology in collaboration with National Mission for Clean Ganga aims to bring together innovative minds to develop solutions integrating emerging technologies with LiDaR technology for conservation, preservation & rejuvenation of our rivers.

What is Riverathon 1.0?

Join a dynamic community of river enthusiasts - including students, researchers, and technologists - united by a common goal: to tackle the pressing challenges facing our rivers. Through the use of advanced LiDAR technology, Riverathon invites participants to map, analyze, and craft forward-thinking solutions aimed at creating a healthier and more resilient river ecosystem. From flood prevention to biodiversity enhancement, your ideas can make a lasting impact.

This is more than just a competition - it’s a movement. Riverathon offers a vibrant platform to collaborate with like-minded innovators, sharpen your skills, and contribute to meaningful environmental change. It's your opportunity to combine creativity with purpose and play an active role in shaping the future of river conservation.

Riverathon 1.0, the flagship event under the Namami Gange Programme, is the first-ever hackathon dedicated to rivers using LiDAR data provided by the Survey of India. It brings together diverse minds from engineering, environmental science, data analytics, and innovation to reimagine the future of our rivers.

At its core, Riverathon promotes a multidisciplinary approach that blends cutting-edge technology with policy insights and community engagement - all aligned with the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals. By participating, you’re not just competing - you’re contributing to water conservation, climate resilience, and the revival of our ecosystems.

Themes

All it takes is one idea to solve an impossible problem.

Floodplain Restoration

Using LiDAR to understand flood dynamics and propose strategies to restore natural floodplains.

Sediment Management

Identifying sediment deposition and erosion patterns for effective riverbed and riverbank.

Biodiversity Mapping

Leveraging LiDAR to analyze riparian zones and support biodiversity conservation initiatives.

Disaster Management

Developing early warning systems for floods, landslides, and other river-related natural disasters.

Riparian Zone/Vegetation Analysis

Analyze riparian vegetation dynamics, assess ecological health, and develop strategies for riparian zone restoration and management.

River Neighborhood Infrastructure Mapping

Identify vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure, assess flood risks, and develop strategies.

Digital Twin for Urban-River Interaction

Create virtual representations of urban-river systems to improve understanding and management.

Climate and Environmental Monitoring

Track the impacts of climate change on river ecosystems and develop adaptation strategies.

TARGET AUDIENCE

Riverathon welcomes participation from students, researchers, and innovators who are deeply committed to environmental sustainability, technological innovation, and impactful problem-solving.

• Full-time undergraduate students enrolled in any discipline at recognized universities or institutions across India.
• Full-time postgraduate students enrolled in any discipline at recognized universities or institutions across India.

All applicants must be Indian nationals and are required to provide valid proof of nationality, such as an Aadhaar card, Passport, Voter ID, or any other government-issued identification document.
